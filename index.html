Geo-designs for the Livingry Crisis


(December 2025 - Mendoza, Argentina)

My work seeks to bridge the gap between geo-designs across different scales of time and industry. The structural X emerges through Design Science as the art of aligning human 'livingry' with these universal cycles." Operating at the intersection of Earth systems' two main vectors: the Logistic Box and the Ecological X. As a practitioner, I recognize that the 'Box' — the rigid standard of global transport and industrial production—is the necessary vehicle for scalability. However, as a Design Scientist, I am committed to 'opening' that box. Through the Structural X, I optimize material mass (Ephemeralization) and create the apertures required for life-cycle performance. By synthesizing the material science of Greg Norris and the energy modeling of Soala Ajienka, I transform the image of the detail and the static dwelling into a high-performance metabolism. I seek to bring this rigorous, performance-first approach to the Harvard GSD to bridge the disciplines of Design, Engineering, and Planetary Stewardship."


The Fulcrum House (The Metabolism)


This entry focuses on the Pragmatic Rigor of the box within the Industrial Inventory. Reminiscent of Fuller grin-silo houses, this investigation leverages the existing industrial machinery to fulfill ordinary needs to solve a housing crisis, such as efficient transportation, regulations and crane-lift capacities.
